# CEO — Baazaar — devdash CEO view evaluation

## Who I am
I'm the co-founder of Baazaar, a Lahore-based e-comm outfit — 30 devs, 80 staff, $120k/month burn, a VC and a family office breathing down my neck. I'm not an engineer; I want to log in Monday morning and know in 10 seconds whether this week is fine or on fire, and what I personally have to unblock so my VPs stop hiding behind "it depends."

## What I like (don't remove)
- **Portfolio health banner** — green/yellow/red counts with "worst-case launch" is exactly how I think about it. One glance, four squads, done.
- **Decisions on you counter** (top right, with the pulsing bell) — this is the best thing on the page. It tells me the company is waiting on ME, not the other way around. Keep the "Xd" age on each one.
- **Clickable project cards → detail modal** — I don't have to ask the PM for scope/phases/risks, it's right there.
- **Team off-project hours tile with the plain-English line** ("Heavy drain — legacy + escalations") — that sentence does more work than any chart.
- **Absent team members callout** — saves me from yelling at someone on leave. Small, specific, useful.
- **The 4 stat tiles jumping straight into QA / PM view** — no hunting through menus.

## What I wish existed
- **Revenue / GMV tile** — I'm burning $120k/month. Show me this week's GMV, conversion rate, AOV next to the dev stats. Dev velocity without revenue context is useless to a board.
- **Runway + payroll-vs-shipped ratio** — "You spent 30 dev-weeks this month, shipped X features, burned $Y." One line.
- **"What shipped to customers this week"** — not commits, not PRs. Features live in production Karachi warehouse + Lahore HQ can actually use.
- **Launch-date trend line** — is the worst-case launch date moving closer or further every week? A sparkline next to the banner would tell me if my VP is sandbagging.
- **Board-ready export / "email this to investors"** — one click → clean PDF or a link I can paste in the WhatsApp group with my board.
- **Cost-per-feature or cost-per-bug** — PKR amount next to each project. I need to know if Squad 3 is costing me 4× what Squad 1 does.
- **Dev attrition / flight-risk signal** — in Pakistan the best devs get poached every 6 months. Who hasn't committed in 2 weeks? Who's gone quiet in handoffs?
- **"Decision debt" aging buckets** — show me the 14d+ ones in red. Right now all decisions look equally scary.
- **Customer-escalation tile** — how many warehouse/ops tickets bounced to dev this week. I need a number to hit my COO with.
- **Mobile view / WhatsApp digest** — I live in WhatsApp. A 4-line auto-summary at 9am would replace 80% of why I open this.

## What's confusing or hard to read
- **"Standout this week" with TRUE NORTH pill** — what does "Hit 75+ on all four directions" mean to me? I don't know what the four directions are without clicking. Label them inline: "shipped a lot, clean code, reliable, owns it."
- **"Readiness: X%" on project cards vs "X% complete"** — two different percentages on the same card. Which one do I quote to the board? Pick one for the CEO view.
- **"Handoff" / "compass" / "True North"** — this is dev-team vocabulary leaking into my view. I want outcomes: "Asif shipped 5 things, 0 bugs."
- **Worst-case launch shows a project name + date but no context** — is 14d late bad? For what project? Why? One more sentence underneath would save me a click.
- **Off-project hours shown as a raw number (e.g. 24h)** — 24 across 30 devs is nothing; 24 for one dev is a crisis. Show it as % of capacity or a per-squad breakdown.
- **Decision-debt card shows the first 3 titles truncated** — I can't read half of them. If they're waiting on me, show me the full title.

## What's noise I'd delete
- **The pixel-phone mascot + CRT ticker** — cute for a dev tool, looks unserious on a board screenshot. Toggle it off by default for the CEO role.
- **The density toggle and 3 themes** — I'll pick one, you can hide the rest in a settings menu. It's pixel real-estate.
- **"Filter dashboard" tiny link on each project card** — duplicates what clicking the card does. Pick one interaction.
- **"Nothing waiting on you. Rare." easter egg** — charming once, patronising on week 4.

## My top 3 complaints (plain English, as if venting to my co-founder)
1. This "Compass" thing with the four directions and the little radar polygon — nobody on my board knows what Velocity vs Craft vs Reliability vs Drive means, and honestly I'd have to squint to explain it either. Just tell me "Asif shipped 5 things this week, 2 were late, 0 came back as bugs." Numbers. Sentences. Not a compass.
2. There is not a single rupee, dirham, or dollar on this entire page. I'm running a business, not a GitHub profile. Where's GMV, where's runway, where's cost-per-squad? This looks like a dashboard for engineers to feel good, not one that helps me defend a $120k burn to the family office.
3. The placeholder numbers aside — I can't tell at a glance if "worst-case launch 14d late" is a catastrophe or a rounding error. No trend, no previous week comparison, no "last week this number was X." A CEO dashboard without a week-over-week arrow is a photograph when I need a video.

## One feature that would make me LOVE it
A **"Monday CEO one-pager"** at the very top — 4 numbers, each with a week-over-week arrow: GMV this week, runway months, features shipped to prod, decisions on me. Below it, 3 auto-generated bullets in plain Urdu-English: *"Checkout migration slipped 5 days because Salman was on leave. Regional VP still hasn't approved the pricing change from 9 days ago. Customer-escalation hours doubled — warehouse sync is breaking."* That would replace my Monday morning WhatsApp barrage to 5 VPs, the weekly status meeting where everyone lies, and half the reason I hired a Chief of Staff. Everything else on this page can stay, but this one strip at the top is what I'd actually pay for.

## Gut-check score
- Signal density (1-5): **3** — "In 10 seconds, can I tell if this week is fine or on fire?" — yes for dev, no for business. Traffic lights land, but no money / no trendline means I'm still guessing.
- Trust (1-5): **3** — "Do I believe the numbers?" — the counts look real, but placeholder data + no data source shown next to each tile means I'd ask "where did this come from?" every Monday until someone answers.
- Time-to-action (1-5): **4** — "Can I act on what I see without asking 3 people?" — the Decisions-on-you inbox and the stat-tile jumps are genuinely good. Loses a point because project detail is 2 clicks and there's no "email this to the owner" button.
- **Would I show this to my board: no** — the compass, the pixel mascot, and the lack of any revenue/runway number make it look like an internal ops tool. Strip 30% of the chrome and add 4 business numbers and yes, I'd screenshot it every month.
